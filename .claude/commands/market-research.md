# Market Research

## Standards
1. Source every material claim
2. Prioritize current data — flag anything > 2 years old
3. Include counterarguments and downside risks
4. Drive toward actionable decisions
5. Distinguish: fact vs inference vs recommendation

## Research Types

### Competitive Analysis
For each competitor:
```markdown
**[Company Name]**
- Product reality (what it actually does, not marketing copy)
- Funding & stage
- Pricing model
- Estimated traction (App Store ratings, Alexa rank, LinkedIn headcount)
- Key strengths
- Known weaknesses / negative reviews
- Distribution channels
- Positioning (who they target)
```
Sources: Company website, G2/Capterra reviews, Crunchbase, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Reddit

### Market Sizing
```markdown
**Total Addressable Market (TAM)**
- Top-down: [industry report source] estimates market at $X
- Bottom-up: [X million potential customers] × [$Y average spend] = $Z

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)**
- Subset TAM actually reachable with your go-to-market
- Constrained by: geography, language, segment, channel

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)**
- Realistic 3-year target: X% of SAM
- Basis: comparable company growth rates, your distribution advantages
```
Always show your math. Label top-down estimates as estimates.

### Technology Research
For any technology decision:
```markdown
**Option: [Technology Name]**
- How it works (mechanics, not marketing)
- Trade-offs vs alternatives
- Integration complexity (1-10)
- Operational risks (hosting, vendor lock-in, scaling)
- Community health (GitHub stars, last commit, Stack Overflow activity)
- License
- Cost at scale
```

### Investor Diligence
```markdown
**[Fund Name]**
- Fund size and vintage
- Check size range
- Stage focus (pre-seed/seed/Series A/B)
- Sector thesis
- Portfolio companies in your space (conflict check)
- Partner who covers your sector
- Known red flags or deal-breakers from portfolio founder feedback
```

## Delivery Structure
```markdown
## Executive Summary
[Decision-enabling 2-3 sentences]

## Key Findings
[Themed sections with evidence]

## Implications
[What this means for your decision]

## Risks & Caveats
[What might be wrong or change]

## Recommendation
[Clear, defensible conclusion]

## Sources
[Numbered, with date accessed]
```

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] All numbers sourced or labeled as estimates
- [ ] Stale data (> 2 years) flagged
- [ ] Recommendations are evidence-based
- [ ] Risks included
- [ ] Output enables a specific decision
- [ ] Counterarguments addressed
